**Subject:** Submission of Manuscript: "Adaptive Wildfire Governance Through Reputation-Weighted Response Networks"

Dear Editor-in-Chief,

I am pleased to submit my manuscript entitled "Adaptive Wildfire Governance Through Reputation-Weighted Response Networks" for consideration for publication.

This paper proposes a governance framework for wildfire response that uses reputation-weighted coordination among distributed response actors. The model combines reputation scoring, resource allocation, and a circulation-inspired participation incentive to improve the timeliness, resilience, and accountability of wildfire governance in high-risk landscapes. A lightweight simulation of representative fire-management agencies demonstrates how reputation-weighted decision influence can support faster consensus on resource deployment and more transparent post-event evaluation.

My work is particularly relevant to the journal because it addresses critical challenges in crisis response, adaptive governance, and collaborative risk management. The proposed framework offers a novel way to integrate trust-weighted collaboration and accountability into wildfire response networks.

The manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration for publication in any other journal. I confirm that this work is original and that the submission is made in accordance with the journal's editorial policies.

The simulation framework referenced in this paper is available in the public GitHub repository: https://github.com/Dylanthoma5667/spiralblockchain (Naressi, 2026)

I believe this paper makes a significant contribution to wildfire governance scholarship and would be of interest to the readers of your journal. I look forward to your response.
